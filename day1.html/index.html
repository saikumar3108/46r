Prabhas, whose full name is Uppalapati Venkata Suryanarayana Prabhas Raju, is a renowned Indian actor primarily known for his work in Telugu cinema. Born on October 23, 1979, in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, he made his acting debut in 2002 with the film Eeswar. He rose to fame with the romantic action film Varsham (2004), and gradually built a strong fan base with his performances in movies like Chatrapathi, Darling, and Mirchi. Prabhas became a household name across India with his iconic role as Amarendra Baahubali in S. S. Rajamouliâ€™s Baahubali series, which became one of the film franchises in Indian cinema.
He dedicated nearly five years to this epic project, which showcased his commitment and passion for acting. The success of Baahubali transformed him into a pan-India star and one of the highest-paid actors in the country. Known for his humble nature and love for fitness, Prabhas also engages in philanthropic activities. With a growing international fan base, he continues to star in high-budget multilingual films, cementing his position as a leading figure in Indian cinema.
